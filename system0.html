Remains Located

Wednesday, June 14th of 2006.

More remains of the unidentified object have been found in the wilderness of ohio. The remains are more preserved than the last ones. There seems to be a pink substance covering some of the material and eggs of unknown origin were found in good condition for studies. We are yet to uncover what the artifacts found are, but most seem to be man-made objects that were somehow altered by whatever is responsible for the rest of the ship.
Some symbols have been noticed on some artifacts and other remains of the unidentified object. Our linguist Margaret Armstrong is leading an intense research under those symbols, to try and understand what they could mean. Evidence leads to the belief that those symbols seem to work like some sort of ideogram, but further information on them is kept with Margaret.
The eggs are being researched by our biologist Gregory Nunez, in an isolated area of the base to prevent any breaches.

Author: Susan Cole, Lead Researcher

Ideograms and Music

Tuesday, June 20th of 2006.

The ideograms seem to follow a linear pattern, with each line being a phrase and each word relating to the word after it. I have managed to gather some more information and content regarding the language in the cybernetic research done by mister David, one of our very experient technical experts. I did my best to translate each word in a coherent and comprehensive manner, so that the rest of the team can use it to ease their research.
One interesting thing that I have noticed is that each word can easily be transcribed as musical notes, disregarding their octaves. Alien Creatures

Wednesday, June 22nd of 2006.

Three eggs have been found alive and healthy, even after the supposed explosion that led to the destruction of the unidentified object, and the remains of an adult specimen have also been found within the wrecks of it.
The eggs seem to have a similar structure as fish eggs, but also have the resistance of reptilian eggs. But, it does not look like they fit into Earth's animal kingdom. Their body structure seems oddly artificial, even though it is of biological nature. The adult specimen seems to have natural armor covering its body, that is also made of similar materials to its own skin, leading us to believe that these creatures naturally evolved into developing thick armor plates to protect its fragile body.
The creatures also seem to have very developed neurological systems, that also resembles cybernetic components. We have analyzed the necessary requirements for the hatching of the eggs, and built an incubator with the proper necessities for the eggs, to try and hatch one of them.
It is believed that the eggs may hatch in about a week.

Author: Gregory Nunez, Biologist
